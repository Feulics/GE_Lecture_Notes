Summary

  • For a 2 body problem

    • Central body is point mass
    • Orbit is an ellipse (Kepler orbit)
    1. Orbits are ellipses, with sun (central body) in one of the 2 foci
    2. During equal intervals of time, the connecting line between central body and planet (object) sweeps equal areas
    3. Square of orbital period is proportional to cube of the semi major axis of the ellipse

  • Disturbing forces perturb orbit

    • Equatorial bulge of the Earth exerts a moment on the orbit causing it to change orientation in space

  • Special orbits, sun synchronous, repeat orbits
